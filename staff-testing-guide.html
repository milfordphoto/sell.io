Milford Photo Used Department

Staff testing guide

Use this checklist to test the used gear quote app and staff intake dashboard before real customer launch.

Before You Start

  • Use fake customer names, phone numbers, and addresses.
  • Do not ship real gear from a test label.
  • Do not enter real bank information during prototype testing.
  • If anything is confusing, use the staff dashboard feedback box and explain what happened.

Customer Quote Test

  1. Open the customer app at milfordphoto.com/sell.
  2. Add one common item, such as a Canon EOS R5, Nikon Zf, or Sony A7 IV.
  3. Choose a condition and review the cash offer, store credit offer, and free shipping status.
  4. Add a second item so you can test a multi-item order.
  5. Continue to details, choose mail-in or in-store dropoff, and submit the quote.
  6. Confirm the done screen explains the correct next step.

Staff Intake Test

  1. Open the protected staff dashboard.
  2. Sign in with the test staff login.
  3. Search for the test customer name, email, phone, or quote number.
  4. Use the order tabs to switch between each piece of gear in the order.
  5. For each item, mark it received and enter a fake serial number.
  6. Verify included accessories and condition.
  7. Save the item evaluation, then move to the next item.
  8. When every item is evaluated, send the final quote or payment-info email.

Customer Decision Test

  1. For an unchanged quote, confirm the staff app sends the correct payment or store-credit next step.
  2. For an adjusted quote, open the final quote link and accept one item.
  3. Request return on another item if the order has multiple pieces of gear.
  4. Return to the staff dashboard and confirm the order moves to payout or return shipment.
  5. Mark payment sent or return shipped only after the correct customer decision is recorded.

What To Report

  • Any button where the next step is not obvious.
  • Any quote that seems wildly too high or too low.
  • Any missing camera, lens, mount, accessory, or condition option.
  • Any customer email or screen that does not clearly explain what happens next.
  • Any page that is hard to use on an iPad or phone.
